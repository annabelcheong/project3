Global Happiness Story

The happiness analysis is based on the dataset from 2020 and 2021 whereby results were taken from people across the world.

Features Affecting Happiness

Below is the list for the 15 happiest countries and the 15 unhappiest countries around the world.
- Each data point represents a country.
- Each feature graph holds 150 data points (i.e. 150 countries)

Click on the specific country to see the country's feature values: GDP per capita, social support, life expectency, freedom, generosity and the perception of corruption.

Analysis Overview:

Features Affecting Happiness graphs

Evidently, the happiest scoring countries are located in Europe and the unhappiest in Africa. There are a few exceptions; with Canada, Australia and New Zealand making the top 15 happy list. The features graphs indicate that there are minimal differences between 2020 and 2021 data.

The main feature which tends to be affecting happiness is GDP per capita. It is clear that higher GDP/capita countries are happier. Other features which exhibit a positive correlation to happiness are Social Support, Life Expectency and Freedom.

Generosity and Perceptions of Corruption tends to not exhibit much of a correlation to happiness.
